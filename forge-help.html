Overview

The Character Forge is a step-by-step character builder for Savage Worlds. It walks you through creation from concept to completed character sheet, with live validation at every stage to ensure your build is rules-legal.

The Forge is designed for building player characters and pre-generated characters from scratch. If you need to manage a roster of NPCs, named characters, or pre-gens across a product line, see the companion tool ‚Äî the Character Vault.

No login is required. The Forge runs entirely in your browser. Character data is held in memory during your session and exported when you're done ‚Äî it is not saved between sessions unless you export before closing the tab.

The Forge does not auto-save. Always export your character before closing the browser. Use JSON export if you plan to continue editing later.

Quick Start

1 Enter a name and concept. Enable any setting modules you want (Ammaria, Saltlands, etc.).

2 Pick an ancestry. Each comes with different racial abilities and starting modifiers.

3 Assign attributes ‚Äî you have 5 points. Each die step above d4 costs one point.

4 Assign skills ‚Äî you have 12 points. Skills linked to higher attributes are cheaper.

5 Choose hindrances to earn extra edges, attribute points, skill points, or starting funds. Then pick your edges.

6 If you took Arcane Background, select your trapping and powers.

7 Buy gear with your starting funds ($500 base, modified by hindrances).

8 Review the summary ‚Äî green indicators mean legal, red means something needs fixing.

Hit Export and pick your format. Done.

Setting Modules

Core Savage Worlds rules are active by default. Setting modules add ancestries, edges, hindrances, and other options specific to a region or supplement. Toggle them on or off ‚Äî module content merges into all relevant steps immediately.

The Active Content bar at the bottom shows exactly how many ancestries, edges, hindrances, and powers are currently available.

‚Üê Interface Layout Step 2: Ancestry ‚Üí

Step 2 ‚Äî Ancestry

Select your character's ancestry. Each ancestry card shows its racial abilities, attribute modifiers, and special rules. Ancestries from enabled setting modules appear alongside core options.

When you select an ancestry, any starting attribute modifiers are applied immediately. Some ancestries begin with certain attributes at d6 instead of d4 ‚Äî this is reflected in the Attributes step automatically.

Some ancestries grant free edges, bonus bennies, or special abilities that don't count against your edge budget. These are noted on the ancestry card.
‚Üê Step 1: Concept Step 3: Attributes ‚Üí

Step 3 ‚Äî Attributes

Every character has five attributes: Agility, Smarts, Spirit, Strength, and Vigor. All start at d4. You have 5 attribute points to spend (plus any bonus from hindrances). Each die step costs one point: d4‚Üíd6 costs 1, d6‚Üíd8 costs 1, and so on up to d12.

Click the die buttons to set each attribute. The budget counter shows points spent versus available. Exceeding your budget turns the step red.

Attributes above d12 appear as d12+1, d12+2, etc. These typically come from ancestry bonuses or specific edges rather than point buy.

Attributes determine the base cost of linked skills and feed into derived stats. Vigor affects Toughness, Fighting affects Parry, and Agility-linked skills are cheaper when Agility is high.
‚Üê Step 2: Ancestry Step 4: Skills ‚Üí

Step 4 ‚Äî Skills

You have 12 skill points to spend (plus any bonus from hindrances). Each skill is linked to an attribute. Raising a skill costs 1 point per die step up to the linked attribute's die. Beyond that, each step costs 2 points.

Core skills (Athletics, Common Knowledge, Notice, Persuasion, Stealth) start at d4 for free. All other skills start at d0 ‚Äî you must invest at least 1 point to acquire them.

1 pointEach step up to and including the linked attribute die
2 pointsEach step above the linked attribute die

Step 5 ‚Äî Hindrances & Edges

Hindrances

Hindrances are character flaws that earn you points to spend on benefits. Click a hindrance to toggle it. Some can be taken as Minor or Major ‚Äî a severity selector appears when applicable.

Hindrance points are spent in the allocation panel:

Edges

Your base budget is 1 edge (from character creation) plus ancestry bonus edges and hindrance-allocated edges. Edges are filtered by category ‚Äî Combat, Social, Professional, etc. Each card shows requirements, and edges whose prerequisites aren't met are dimmed.

Some edges are stackable (like Power Points) ‚Äî clicking adds another instance.

‚Üê Step 4: Skills Step 6: Powers ‚Üí

Step 6 ‚Äî Powers

This step only activates if you've taken the Arcane Background edge. Select your arcane background type (Gifted, Magic, Miracles, etc.), then choose powers from the available list.

Starting power count and Power Points depend on your chosen background. New Powers adds 2 more picks; Power Points adds 5 PP per instance.

Each power card shows PP cost, range, duration, and effect summary. Your power budget and PP total are tracked at the top of the panel.

Power trappings are narrative choices rather than mechanical ones in Savage Worlds. The Forge tracks the framework ‚Äî you describe the flavour at the table.
‚Üê Step 5: Hindrances Step 7: Gear ‚Üí

Step 7 ‚Äî Gear

Buy equipment from the gear catalogue. Items are sorted into tabs: Melee, Ranged, Armor, Shields, and General. Click to add, click again to remove. The cost tracker shows your budget in real time.

Starting funds default to $500 (or 500‚Ç° in Ammaria). If you allocated hindrance points to extra funds, this is doubled. Rich gives three times; Filthy Rich gives five times. Poverty halves starting funds.

Gear added here is included in all exports ‚Äî Fantasy Grounds XML, PDF sheets, and stat blocks all reflect your selections with correct damage, armour, weight, and cost values.

‚Üê Step 6: Powers Step 8: Summary ‚Üí

Step 8 ‚Äî Summary

The summary presents your completed character in a read-only stat block format. All attributes, skills, derived stats, edges, hindrances, powers, and gear are displayed. This is the "proofread before you print" step.

Check for red indicators on any step tab (click to jump directly to the problem), derived stats that look wrong (Parry should be 2 + half Fighting die), missing gear or powers, and overall concept coherence.

Setting Modules

Setting modules extend the Forge with content from specific supplements. Each module can add ancestries, edges, hindrances, skills, gear, and arcane backgrounds.

Modules are toggled on the Concept step. Core content is always active. Supplement content is identified by source throughout the builder ‚Äî you'll see tags like (Ammaria) or (Saltlands) next to module-specific options.

When a module is disabled, selections from it are preserved but may cause validation warnings. Re-enable the module to clear these.

Module data is loaded from companion JavaScript files (swade-core.js, ammaria.js, etc.). These are cached by your browser after first load.

Troubleshooting

My character disappeared when I refreshed

The Forge runs in-memory and does not auto-save. Always export (JSON recommended) before closing or refreshing. Lost builds cannot be recovered.

An edge shows as available but shouldn't be

Some prerequisites (like rank requirements beyond Novice) aren't enforced because Novice is the assumed starting rank. Review the edge's requirements text manually.

Gear costs don't match the rulebook

Prices come from module data files. If you're using a setting module, prices may differ from core equipment lists. Check which modules are active.

Import failed with "Invalid JSON"

Ensure you're pasting the complete object, starting with { and ending with }. Use JSON format, not text stat block. HTML and RTF are not importable in the Forge.

PDF export looks wrong

Very long character names or gear lists may cause layout overflow. Try shortening. Ensure portraits are JPG or PNG under 2MB.
