Overview

The Character Forge is a step-by-step character builder for Savage Worlds. It walks you through creation from concept to completed character sheet, with live validation at every stage to ensure your build is rules-legal.

The Forge is designed for building player characters and pre-generated characters from scratch. If you need to manage a roster of NPCs, named characters, or pre-gens across a product line, see the companion tool ‚Äî the Character Vault.

No login is required. The Forge runs entirely in your browser. Character data is held in memory during your session and exported when you're done ‚Äî it is not saved between sessions unless you export before closing the tab.

The Forge does not auto-save. Always export your character before closing the browser. Use JSON export if you plan to continue editing later.

Quick Start

If you just want to get building:

1 Enter a name and concept. Enable any setting modules you want (Ammaria, Saltlands, etc.).

2 Pick an ancestry. Each one comes with different racial abilities and starting modifiers.

3 Assign attributes ‚Äî you have 5 points. Each die step above d4 costs one point.

4 Assign skills ‚Äî you have 12 points. Skills linked to higher attributes are cheaper.

5 Choose hindrances to earn extra edges, attribute points, skill points, or starting funds. Then pick your edges.

6 If you took Arcane Background, select your trapping and powers.

7 Buy gear with your starting funds ($500 base, modified by hindrances).

8 Review the summary ‚Äî green indicators mean legal, red means something needs fixing.

Hit Export and pick your format. Done.

Setting Modules

The Forge ships with Savage Worlds core rules active by default. Setting modules add ancestries, edges, hindrances, and other options specific to a region or supplement. Toggle them on or off as needed ‚Äî the module's content is merged into all relevant steps immediately.

The Active Content bar at the bottom shows you exactly how many ancestries, edges, hindrances, and powers are currently available from your enabled modules.

JSON Import

This step also contains the Import Character panel. You can load a previously exported JSON file (from the Forge or the Character Vault) to continue editing or adapt an existing build. See JSON Transfer for full details.

Importing replaces your entire current build. If you have unsaved work, export it first.

Step 2 ‚Äî Ancestry

Select your character's ancestry. Each ancestry card shows its racial abilities, attribute modifiers, and any special rules. Ancestries from enabled setting modules appear alongside core Savage Worlds options.

When you select an ancestry, any starting attribute modifiers are applied immediately. Some ancestries (like Half-Folk) begin with certain attributes at d6 instead of d4 ‚Äî this is reflected in the Attributes step automatically.

Some ancestries grant free edges, bonus bennies, or special abilities that don't count against your edge budget. These are noted on the ancestry card.

Step 3 ‚Äî Attributes

Every character has five attributes: Agility, Smarts, Spirit, Strength, and Vigor. All start at d4. You have 5 attribute points to spend (plus any bonus points from hindrances). Each die step costs one point: d4‚Üíd6 costs 1, d6‚Üíd8 costs 1, and so on up to d12.

Click the die buttons to set each attribute. The budget counter shows points spent versus available. If you exceed your budget, the step turns red.

Attributes above d12 are shown as d12+1, d12+2, etc. These are rare and typically come from ancestry bonuses or specific edges rather than point buy.

Attributes determine the base cost of linked skills and feed into derived stats. Vigor affects Toughness, Fighting affects Parry, and Agility-linked skills are cheaper when Agility is high.

Step 4 ‚Äî Skills

You have 12 skill points to spend (plus any bonus from hindrances). Each skill is linked to an attribute. Raising a skill costs 1 point per die step up to the linked attribute's die. Beyond that, each step costs 2 points.

Core skills (Athletics, Common Knowledge, Notice, Persuasion, Stealth) start at d4 for free. All other skills start at d0 ‚Äî you must invest at least 1 point to acquire them.

Use the + and ‚àí buttons to step skills up or down. The die size is shown alongside the linked attribute for easy reference. The budget tracker shows your total spend in real time.

Step 5 ‚Äî Hindrances & Edges

Hindrances

Hindrances are character flaws that earn you points to spend on benefits. Click a hindrance to toggle it. Some hindrances can be taken as Minor or Major ‚Äî a severity selector appears when applicable.

Hindrance points are spent in the allocation panel that appears once you've selected at least one hindrance. You can allocate points to:

Edges

Edges are advantages and special abilities. Your base budget is 1 edge (from character creation) plus any ancestry bonus edges and hindrance-allocated edges.

Edges are filtered by category ‚Äî Combat, Social, Professional, etc. Click the category tabs to browse. Each edge card shows its requirements, so you can see at a glance whether your character qualifies. Edges whose requirements aren't met are dimmed.

Some edges are stackable (like Power Points) ‚Äî you can take these multiple times. Clicking a stackable edge adds another instance.

Step 6 ‚Äî Powers

This step only activates if you've taken the Arcane Background edge. Select your arcane background type (Gifted, Magic, Miracles, etc.), then choose your powers from the available list.

The number of starting powers and Power Points depends on your chosen background. Taking the New Powers edge adds 2 more power picks; Power Points adds 5 PP per instance.

Each power card shows its PP cost, range, duration, and effect summary. Your power budget and PP total are tracked at the top of the panel.

Power trappings are a narrative choice rather than a mechanical one in Savage Worlds. The Forge tracks the mechanical framework ‚Äî you describe the flavour at the table.

Step 7 ‚Äî Gear

Buy equipment from the gear catalogue. Items are sorted into tabs: Melee, Ranged, Armor, Shields, and General. Click an item to add it, click again to remove. The cost tracker shows your budget in real time.

Your starting funds default to $500 (or 500‚Ç° in Ammaria). If you allocated hindrance points to extra funds, this is doubled. If you took the Rich edge, you start with three times the base; Filthy Rich gives five times.

The Poverty hindrance halves your starting funds.

Gear added here is included in all exports ‚Äî Fantasy Grounds XML, PDF sheets, and stat blocks all reflect your selections with correct damage, armour, weight, and cost values.

Step 8 ‚Äî Summary

The summary step presents your completed character in a read-only stat block format. All attributes, skills, derived stats, edges, hindrances, powers, and gear are displayed. This is the "proofread before you print" step.

Check for:

‚Ä¢ Red indicators on any step tab (click to jump directly to the problem)
‚Ä¢ Derived stats that look wrong (Parry should be 2 + half your Fighting die)
‚Ä¢ Missing gear or powers you intended to take
‚Ä¢ Overall concept coherence ‚Äî does the build match the character you imagined?

Import on the Concept Step

Two import options appear at the bottom of the Concept step:

Upload JSON ‚Äî click to select a .json file from your filesystem. The Forge reads it, validates the format, and loads all fields into the builder. You'll land on the Summary step so you can review the imported build.

Paste JSON ‚Äî opens a text prompt where you can paste raw JSON (useful if you copied from the Vault's clipboard export). Same validation and loading process.

Importing replaces your entire current build. If you have unsaved work, export it first. The Forge will ask for confirmation if you already have a character name entered.

Setting Modules

Setting modules extend the Forge with content from specific supplements. Each module can add ancestries, edges, hindrances, skills, gear, and arcane backgrounds to the builder.

Modules are toggled on the Concept step. Core Savage Worlds content is always active. Supplement content is identified by source throughout the builder ‚Äî you'll see tags like (Ammaria) or (Saltlands) next to module-specific options.

When a module is disabled, any selections from that module are preserved in your build data but may cause validation warnings. Re-enable the module to clear these.

Module data is loaded from the companion JavaScript files (swade-core.js, ammaria.js, etc.). These are cached by your browser and don't require a network connection after first load.

Troubleshooting

My character disappeared when I refreshed the page

The Forge runs in-memory and does not auto-save. Always export (JSON recommended) before closing or refreshing. If you've lost a build, it cannot be recovered.

An edge shows as available but shouldn't be

Edge requirements are checked against your current selections. Some prerequisites (like rank requirements beyond Novice) aren't enforced in the builder because Novice is the assumed starting rank. Review the edge's requirements text manually.

Gear costs don't match the rulebook

Gear prices come from the module data files. If you're using a setting module, prices may differ from the core Savage Worlds equipment list. Check which modules are active on the Concept step.

Import failed with "Invalid JSON"

Ensure you're pasting the complete JSON object, starting with { and ending with }. If you exported from the Vault, make sure you used the JSON format, not the text stat block. HTML and RTF are not importable in the Forge.

The PDF export looks wrong or has missing sections

PDF generation uses the jsPDF library. Very long character names or extremely long gear lists may cause layout overflow. Try shortening the name or concept. If the portrait is missing, ensure it's a JPG or PNG under 2MB.
